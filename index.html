About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Joseph Fourier University, working in the PACSS group at the laboratory Verimag. I have completed my PhD at Lorraine University, in the CARTE group at the laboratory Loria.

My research focuses on the binary code analysis and on the detection of malwares. I am interested in formal models of programs and their applications in extracting high-level properties of programs from obfuscated binary codes.

In my spare time, I do reverse engineering and write some write-ups. I write also some self-modifying code challenges.
