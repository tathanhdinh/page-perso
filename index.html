Thanh Dinh Ta

About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Joseph Fourier University, working in PACSS group at Verimag laboratory. I currently participate in BinSec project to design and implement a guided dynamic symbolic execution engine, and to study a formal approach to deobfuscate virtualization-obfsucated binaries.

Before joining PACSS, I have completed my PhD at Lorraine University, in CARTE group at Loria laboratory. My thesis is about a formal model for obfuscating transformations on behaviors of programs, and about a semantics-based method to synthensizing hidden automata which are used to parse received messages of obfuscated binaries.

My research focuses on automated methods for computer security, particularly for binary code analysis and malware detection. I am interested in formal models of programs and their applications in extracting high-level similarities between obfuscated programs.
